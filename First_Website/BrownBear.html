THE BROWN BEAR

About Brown Bears :

The brown bear (Ursus arctos) is native to parts of northern Eurasia and North America. Its conservation status is currently Least Concerned.

There are many subspecies within the brown bear bloodline, these subspecies include the Atlas bear and the Himalayan brown bear.

Species :

  • Arctos
  • Collarus
  • Horribillis
  • Nelsoni (Extinct)

Features :

The brown bear has a slight hump above its shoulder, round ears, a long snout and big paws with long, curved claws that it uses for digging. Unlike the black bear, it can't climb trees. It can weigh between 350-1,500 pounds.

Habitat :

Brown bears can be found in many habitats, from the fringes of deserts to high mountain forests and ice fields. In Europe, the brown bear is mostly found in mountain woodlands.

Countries with the most brown bear population :

The countries that has the most brown bear population is Russia, with a total of 120,000. This is followed up by the US with 32,500 bears, and rougly 25,000 bears in Canada.

Countries with the least brown bear population :

Countries with smaller brown bear populations include Armenia, Belarus, Bulgaria, China, Finland, France, Greece, India, Japan, Nepal, Poland, Romania, Slovenia, Turkmenistan, and Uzbekistan.
